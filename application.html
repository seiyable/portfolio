Project Theme:

A Design of Future Partner Mobility That Draws Empathic Interactions

Background of This Project:

This “Robotic Service Design (RSD)” project is launched by economics professor Masanao Takeyama in Keio University and Toyota Central R&D Labs, Inc. We conduct research activities from the perspective of the users aimed to depict how robot technologies will be used 15 years from now. Especially, we focus on the possibilities that will bring the spread of personal mobility in the near future. By integrating robotic technologies, we attempt to propose an unprecedented fascinating urban mobility-life.

Concept of Our Proposal:

The past robots are recognized as the image of “smart machines that can be entrusted human tasks to”. There is a history of technological innovation that human have demanded to be released themselves from some sort of labor on the background. However, in our RSD project, we consider the robotics not as the technology that does something to help us, but rather as the technology that skillfully leverages human being abilities. Human being have a habit that they cannot help but sense “life” and its “intention” to something moving; therefore, by utilizing the habit, we expect the possibilities of robotics could let human beings to empathize with robots, and lead to new behaviors for human beings.
